## Background

NSM (Wierzbicka & Goddard, 2014) identifies ~65 semantic primes — concepts that exist in all human languages and cannot be defined in terms of simpler concepts. The Sistema 7×7 (La Danza Cósmica de los Opuestos) independently derives 63 ontological primitives from a philosophical framework based on dimensional emergence (0D→3D+).

These two systems were developed completely independently:
- **NSM**: 40+ years of cross-linguistic fieldwork across 30+ languages
- **Sistema 7×7**: Philosophical ontology based on dimensional symmetry and opposition

The convergence between them provides strong evidence that both capture genuine universals of human conceptual structure.

## Interpretation

The 55% overlap between two independently derived systems is remarkable:

1. **Core cognitive predicates converge**: THINK, KNOW, WANT, SEE, HEAR, SAY, DO, MOVE — both systems identify the same mental and action primitives.

2. **Logical/modal concepts converge**: BECAUSE, IF, CAN, MAYBE — both systems identify the same causal and modal structure.

3. **Quantification converges**: ONE, SOME, ALL, MANY, MORE, PART, KIND — nearly identical inventories.

4. **Divergences are principled**: NSM includes deictic/indexical primes (I, YOU, THIS, HERE, NOW) that are linguistic necessities but not ontological primitives. Sistema 7×7 includes phenomenological/observer primitives (consciousness, elements, hedonic poles) that are ontologically motivated but not cross-linguistic universals.

5. **The systems are complementary**: NSM captures what is *linguistically irreducible*; Sistema 7×7 captures what is *ontologically irreducible*. Their overlap identifies concepts that are both.
